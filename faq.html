Tools of the trade

Tablet:

Wacom intuos pro medium - I personally like it a lot over the screen tablets, simply for ergonomical reasons. I haven't had a major wrist injury since I used this tablet.

Program:

Clip Studio Paint - By far my favorite program to work with! I started out with Photoshop for 1 year before turning to Clip Studio Paint, and I can say with confidence that I found Clip Studio Paint to be superior for art. But this is of course personal preference.

Learning resources:

Tutorials - Over the years I have followed many tutorial, A few that stood out to me are:

• Proko: Has an amazing 15 hour+ figure drawing course and was well worth it for me! If you have doubts, I highly recommend checking out his videos on youtube about figure drawing / portrait drawing to see if you like his teaching style. • Brent Eviston: Has a great course on Udemy called, "The Art & Science of Figure Drawing: Gesture". It's a short and nice course with lots of exercises. • New Masters Academy: You probably won't find another website with such talented fine artists that teach you the ways of figure drawing, portrait drawing, and many other things. I especially enjoyed the classes from 'Steve Huston'. Observation - When I was learning, there weren't a lot of anime tutorials and resources out there. Meaning that, to learn, observation has always been one of the most important skills.

Tracing studies - But to really understand a subject, you will also have to dissect it into simple steps. With digital art, this has become a lot easier! Just simply lower the opacity of one of your favorite pieces, and find the major shapes. After doing this a few time, try it from your imagination!
